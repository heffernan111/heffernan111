About

I'm a software engineer, field scientist, and postgraduate astrophysicist with a passion for real-time systems, off-grid autonomy, and expedition-based science. I’ve spent the last 12 years building distributed platforms across industry and academia; from Laravel-based control interfaces for robotic telescopes to Rust-based telemetry dashboards decoding satellite signals in real time.

My professional path bridges observatory software, mission control systems, and DevOps engineering. But I’m just as comfortable in a kayak or remote field station as I am behind a terminal. My recent analogue astronaut expeditions have strengthened my belief that the future of science and exploration lies at the intersection of extreme environments, autonomy, and human adaptability.

I also work with embedded Python in scientific systems — building lightweight automation pipelines, data reduction tools, and interactive telemetry interfaces in harsh environments. This includes scripting onboard telescope controls and remote sensing systems under hardware and bandwidth constraints.

Aspirations

I’m aiming toward a PhD in either solar physics, expeditionary sensing, or large-scale cosmological structure; ideally projects that combine data, software, and field deployment. Long-term, I hope to contribute to analogue mission training, Earth-space systems engineering, and one day, astronaut selection itself.

I'm particularly interested in the Hubble tension; the discrepancy between local and early-universe expansion rate measurements. My recent MSc work explores alternative redshift-distance relations and the possibility of wavelength-dependent propagation effects as a solution, bridging theory with observational astrophysics.

My technical focus is on building resilient, observable systems for science in motion; be it an ocean-borne environmental platform, a satellite-ground telemetry bridge, or a remote-control stack for an isolated telescope.

If you're working in expedition science, wind dynamics, cosmology, or astronomical instrumentation, I’d love to connect.
