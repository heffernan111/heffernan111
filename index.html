About

My professional path bridges observatory software, mission control systems, and DevOps engineering. I've built distributed platforms across industry and academia - from Laravel-based control interfaces for robotic telescopes to Rust-based telemetry dashboards decoding satellite signals in real time.

But I'm just as comfortable in a kayak or remote field station as I am behind a terminal. My analogue astronaut expeditions have strengthened my belief that the future of science and exploration lies at the intersection of extreme environments, autonomy, and human adaptability.

I also work with embedded Python in scientific systems - building lightweight automation pipelines, data reduction tools, and interactive telemetry interfaces under hardware and bandwidth constraints.

Aspirations

I am aiming for a PhD in cosmology or space engineering, with a focus on projects that integrate observational data, instrumentation, and software for large-scale scientific investigations.

I'm particularly interested in the Hubble tension: the discrepancy between local and early-universe expansion rate measurements. My recent MSc work explores alternative redshift-distance relations and wavelength-dependent propagation effects.

My technical focus is on building resilient, observable systems for science in motion - whether an ocean-borne environmental platform, a satellite-ground telemetry bridge, or a remote-control stack for an isolated telescope.

If you're working in expedition science, wind dynamics, cosmology, or astronomical instrumentation - I'd love to connect.
